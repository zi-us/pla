Our Research Team

Leading scientists and researchers pushing the boundaries of plasma physics understanding

Dr. Li Zhang

Director of Plasma Physics Research

Dr. Zhang leads the Zi-US plasma physics research division with over 20 years of experience in fusion energy and computational plasma modeling. Her pioneering work on nonlinear Debye shielding has revolutionized our understanding of high-density plasma behavior. She received her Ph.D. from MIT and has published over 150 peer-reviewed papers in leading physics journals.

Current research focuses on developing advanced mathematical frameworks for plasma turbulence and applying machine learning techniques to fusion reactor optimization. Dr. Zhang serves on the IAEA International Fusion Research Council and is a fellow of the American Physical Society.

Dr. James Smith

Senior Research Scientist

Specializes in experimental plasma diagnostics and laser-plasma interactions. Leads the development of novel measurement techniques for high-temperature plasma characterization.

Dr. Maria Rodriguez

Computational Physics Lead

Expert in particle-in-cell simulations and GPU-accelerated plasma modeling. Develops cutting-edge computational tools for studying collective phenomena in magnetized plasmas.

Dr. Takeshi Nakamura

Industrial Applications Lead

Bridges fundamental plasma research with semiconductor manufacturing applications. Pioneered new plasma etching techniques adopted by major chip manufacturers.

Dr. Sarah Chen

Postdoctoral Researcher

Investigating quantum effects in ultra-cold plasmas and developing novel cooling techniques for ion trap quantum computers using plasma physics principles.

Dr. Michael Anderson

Fusion Energy Specialist

Focuses on tokamak plasma stability and disruption mitigation strategies. Collaborates with ITER and private fusion companies on control system development.

Dr. Elena Petrov

Graduate Student

Ph.D. candidate studying nonlinear wave propagation in dusty plasmas. Developing theoretical models for space plasma environments.

Research Groups

Specialized teams advancing different aspects of plasma physics

Theoretical Plasma Physics

Developing mathematical frameworks and analytical models for understanding plasma dynamics, turbulence, and transport phenomena in various plasma regimes.

Computational Plasma Physics

Utilizing supercomputing resources to simulate complex plasma phenomena, from microscopic kinetic effects to macroscopic fluid behavior.

6 members • Led by Dr. Maria Rodriguez

Experimental Diagnostics

Designing and implementing advanced measurement techniques for characterizing plasma properties in extreme conditions.

5 members • Led by Dr. James Smith

Applied Plasma Technology
